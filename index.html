WELL I'M STILL WORKING ON IT

There should probably be a good reason why I don't share or post any of my pictures and as it seems, there isn't. So as not to completely foil my previous stance on social media I created a website of the images I have taken of our family over the last few years. Certainly, there are many more photos than are available here but for the most part, the ones worth sharing are available to view.

 

Since nobody likes slow web pages, me especially, the resolution and quality of the photos are lowered significantly from the originals to reduce load times. If you would like to have or view the originals they are available here, although the file is rather large. A lower resolution download can be found here, it contains all of the exact images you will see on this site.

The images are organized into the four 'collections' or perhaps albums if you prefer. The collections are not the best style of organization but merely the most convenient.

 

In the future, it might be nice to have a feature for everyone to upload their photos for a certain event so we can all view the full collection of photos. Instead of paying for server space, however, theres a novel little startup out of Boston that seems to take care of this conglomeration of photos. It's just unlikely I will be contributing...

 

Much like popular social media sites, this website has an ad of its own. I expect it is less distracting and better audience targeted than those on Facebook.

Winter

This Winter break I am going to the North West part of Ecuador to create a construction company providing sustainable bamboo housing to replace those buildings demolished in a 7.8 magnitude quake that struck Bahia last summer. I will be going with a team of UC Berkeley students and meeting up with Architecture professors from all over the world in Bahia, Ecuador.  There is currently a very high need for a housing company as the current building practices are clearly not fit for the environment. We will be creating the company headquarters building during our stay in Bahia and teaching the local community members how to build long lasting bamboo structures. When we leave we will be giving the company to the community as a cooperative. In order to initially create the company we are raising the funds ourselves through usual fund raising techniques as well as crowd funding.   If you would like to find out more I encourage you to go to our website below, or if you would be so kind as to donate to the cause, a link to my GoFundMe is provided as well.
